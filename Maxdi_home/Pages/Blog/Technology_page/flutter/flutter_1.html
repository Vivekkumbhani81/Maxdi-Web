Factors We Consider When Determining Flutter App Development Cost 

After several discussions with our development heads to understand how things work, we have come up with the most important aspects that contribute to the total cost of developing applications. This might help you during discussions with your development partner. 

1. App Complexity 

Applications are generally categorized into three, based on their level of complexity: low, medium, and high complexity apps. More and more complicated features means higher complexity of the apps. 

Additionally, the requirement for various functionalities, third-party integrations, deployment architecture, and use of modern technologies (AI-ML, Blockchain, etc.) contribute to an appâ€™s overall complexity. 

Complexity of features and functionalities differs from app to app. For example, building a simple fitness app that provides meals and workout plans will require less complex features than a stock trading app that has complicated functionalities and government regulations to follow.

2. App Niche/Industry 

Application requirements vary across industries. Therefore, apps can be divided into different categories based on user demographics, crucial functionalities, special features, level of security, legal regulations, etc. 

When industry/app categories change, you may notice that feature sets and functionalities vary significantly. For example, a banking app requires two-factor authentication while a simple gaming app may not even require login.  

Thus, the overall cost of developing a Flutter app for your business majorly depends on the industry youâ€™re operating in. 

3. Design Requirements & Approach 

Despite a great app idea, design (UI/UX) plays a major role in getting desired success. While you would want an intricate, free-flowing, and intuitive app design and topography, itâ€™s a significant factor that affects the overall app development expenditure.  

With your development partner and their design team, you can come up with ways to keep the design minimal yet interactive. The estimated cost for designing your flutter app varies according to complexity and design approach. 

  • Simple app design: $200 to $1,000 
  • Medium complexity app design: $1,000 to $5,000 
  • Highly-complex app design: $5,000 to $10,000 

However, there are various other factors like number of screens, animation, use of custom icons, etc that impact the overall cost in the design phase.  

4. Additional Tools & Licensing Costs 

Flutter is an open-source framework. However, in order to build your perfect enterprise applications, development teams may incorporate additional tools into the process. 

The use of IDEs (Integrated Development Environment) and third-party API integrations for programming or payments require licensing and payment. Thus, these factors add-up to the overall Flutter app development cost.  

5. Server Space Requirements 

Enough server space is concerned for seamless functioning of apps that need to process and store data. Less server space often results in lagging apps that leave the users frustrated. Additionally, one needs to make sure that the servers theyâ€™re using are secure enough to avoid any data leaks or breaches. 

You may have to pay around $1500/ year for a 24-hour working server with a high RAM space and reliable CPU. However, there are options for customization which makes space for variation in the actual costs.  

6. Testing & QA 

Testing and QA (Quality Assurance) play a crucial role in launching a flawless, robust app. No one wants that a great appâ€™s performance gets ruined by a bug or error. To make sure that your app functions seamlessly across focused platforms and devices, testing and quality assurance should never be skipped. 

While QA and testing is a part of the development process, the different approaches and time-frames result in varying costs. As per general estimates, testing and QA costs are 10%-25% of total development cost. 

7. App Release Costs 

Once the development of the Flutter app is complete, it should be released on respective app stores. Considering your focus on iOS and Android, certainly you would need to deploy your application on Apple App Store and Google Play Store. These platforms charge a one-time registration fee for each app launch. 

The costs associated with launching an app on Google Play:

  • One-time $25 registration fee
  • 15% service fee on the first $1 million in earnings each year
  • 30% service fee on earnings over $1 million each year

The Apple Store app launch charge is $99 per year. This fee is required for all developers who want to publish their apps on the App Store. The fee is due each year, regardless of how many apps you publish or how many downloads they receive. 

Just like Google Play Apple also charges a commission on all paid app sales and in-app purchases. The commission rate is 30% for the first $1 million in earnings, and 15% for all earnings over $1 million. 

8. Post Launch Maintenance 

The secret ingredient to the success of most apps is regular update and maintenance post launch. According to usersâ€™ feedback and demand you may need to upgrade your app features from time to time. 

There are also other technological updates that remain unavoidable while ensuring smooth functioning of the application. So majorly there will be general app updates, design changes, and bug fixes to deal with. 

You may need 15%-20% of the overall development cost to maintain and update your app in the future. Considering these additional costs when calculating Flutter app development cost, helps you set a clear and sufficient budget bar.
